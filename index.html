welcome

Hi!

I am a first-year graduate student in the Department of Cognitive Science at Johns Hopkins University. My advisors are Paul Smolensky and Géraldine Legendre.

I finished my undergraduate degree in computer science and linguistics at Truman State University in spring of 2014 and began the PhD program at Hopkins the following fall.

Research Interests

My research interests are broad, but mostly center around computational cogntive science and linguistics, theoretical linguistics, and language acquisition.

I am currently working closely with Paul Smolensky and others on the gradient symbolic computation project, looking for ways to integrate discrete (symbolic) and continuous (connectionist) models of cognition.
